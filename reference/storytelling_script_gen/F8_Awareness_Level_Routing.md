# F8_Awareness_Level_Routing.md
## Schwartz's 5 Levels of Awareness — The Script Routing Layer
> **Priority:** 🔴 CRITICAL — Determines WHICH narrative framework to deploy BEFORE any writing begins.
> **Source:** Eugene Schwartz, *Breakthrough Advertising* (1966) + modern performance marketing adaptation.
> **Load Order:** 2nd (immediately after F1, before any arc or hook selection).

---

## 1. Strategic Context: Why This Is the Highest-Leverage Addition

Your engine currently produces one script type regardless of where the audience sits on the awareness spectrum. This means:

- An **Unaware** viewer receives product-centric content → rejects it as irrelevant noise
- A **Most Aware** viewer receives extended education → abandons out of impatience
- A **Problem-Aware** viewer receives a solution comparison → overwhelmed by options they didn't ask for

**The Awareness Level is the routing decision that happens BEFORE you choose an arc, a hook, or a CTA.** Without it, even perfectly crafted StoryBrand scripts underperform because they're served to the wrong audience at the wrong time.

---

## 2. The 5 Levels of Awareness

| Level | % of Market | What They Know | What They Need | Their Mindset |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Unaware** | ~60% | Don't know they have a problem | Curiosity — make them NOTICE the problem exists | *"Everything's fine... right?"* |
| **2. Problem-Aware** | ~20% | Know the pain, don't know solutions exist | Validation — mirror their pain, then introduce the solution category | *"Something's wrong, but I don't know what to do about it."* |
| **3. Solution-Aware** | ~10% | Know solutions exist, evaluating categories | Differentiation — show why YOUR methodology/approach wins | *"I know I need X, but which one?"* |
| **4. Product-Aware** | ~7% | Know YOUR product, haven't decided | Evidence — proof it works, risk elimination, social proof | *"I know about you, but convince me."* |
| **5. Most Aware** | ~3% | Ready to buy, need the push | A compelling offer — direct, confident, with urgency or guarantee | *"Tell me the price and terms."* |

## 3. The Master Routing Table

> **This table is the core decision engine.** Consult it BEFORE selecting any content parameters.

| Awareness Level | Narrative Framework | Hook Type | Video Type | CTA Type | Product Mention | StoryBrand Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Unaware** | HSO (Brunson) — Story breaks false belief | Curiosity / Thought Leadership | UGC, Long-form editorial | None / Soft follow (subscribe) | **ZERO** — no product, no brand in first 80% | Story = the entire vehicle. No selling. |
| **Problem-Aware** | PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution) | Empathy Hook — mirror felt pain | Social Teaser, UGC | Transitional — free guide/checklist | Category mention only, no brand | Agitate → introduce solution category |
| **Solution-Aware** | StoryBrand (Full Arc) — Guide + Plan | Challenger Hook — differentiate methodology | Technical Explainer, Brand Manifesto | Transitional — demo/whitepaper/comparison | Brand as Guide, methodology front | Full 7-beat arc deployed |
| **Product-Aware** | PASTOR (Amplify + Testimony) | Data Hook / Proof Hook — evidence | Technical Explainer, Testimonial | Transitional → Direct — PoC/trial | Full product, social proof, case studies | Testimony carries the weight |
| **Most Aware** | Direct Offer (Godfather) — short, confident | Direct Offer — skip the hook | Social Teaser (short), Direct Ad | Direct ("Marry Me") — buy/book/reserve | Direct offer + bold guarantee | Minimal story — offer is the story |

## 4. Framework Details by Level

### 4.1. Unaware → HSO (Hook-Story-Offer)

**Russell Brunson's framework.** The audience doesn't know they have a problem. You can't sell — you can only intrigue.

| Component | Execution |
|---|---|
| **Hook** | Curiosity-driven: *"What if everything you know about X is wrong?"* / Thought leadership: *"I spent 10 years studying Y and here's what nobody talks about."* |
| **Story** | Break the false belief. The story's explicit purpose is to override the viewer's existing worldview. Not positioning — *reframing*. |
| **Offer** | Content only: follow, subscribe, download the guide. ZERO product. The offer is more story. |

**Content Types:** LinkedIn thought leadership, podcast clips, editorial UGC, "myth-busting" threads.
**Metric That Matters:** Engagement rate, follower growth, content saves. NOT conversions.

### 4.2. Problem-Aware → PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

**The audience knows the pain but doesn't know a solution category exists.**

| Component | Execution |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Name it precisely — use their language, not industry terms. Mirror the Internal Problem. |
| **Agitate** | Escalate the emotional discomfort. Future-cast consequences. Ripple-effect mapping. Quantify pain (hours lost, dollars wasted). In video: pacing accelerates, music builds tension. |
| **Solution** | Introduce the solution *category* — not your product. *"There's a class of tools that..."* |

**Content Types:** Social teasers, short-form UGC, "Did you know?" content.
**Metric That Matters:** Save rate, share rate, comment quality. Building problem recognition.

### 4.3. Solution-Aware → StoryBrand (Full 7-Beat Arc)

**This is where your existing engine excels.** The audience knows solutions exist and is evaluating options. Deploy the full Hero/Guide/Plan arc.

| Component | Execution |
|---|---|
| **Hook** | Challenger Hook — break the false belief about alternatives |
| **Foreshadow** | Bold claim or before/after tease |
| **Agitate** | PAS escalation — why the OTHER approaches fail |
| **Guide + Plan** | Brand enters as Guide with 3-step plan |
| **Peak** | Transformation reveal or "aha" insight |
| **CTA** | Transitional — demo, whitepaper, comparison tool |

**Content Types:** Brand Manifesto, Technical Explainer, comparison content.
**Metric That Matters:** Demo requests, whitepaper downloads, time-on-page.

### 4.4. Product-Aware → PASTOR (Problem-Amplify-Story-Testimony-Offer-Response)

**Ray Edwards' framework.** They know your product — they need proof and risk elimination.

| Component | Execution |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Brief — they already know it. Acknowledge, don't belabor. |
| **Amplify** | Quantify the cost of inaction (Kahneman Prospect Theory). Aspirational contrast: where they are vs. where they'd be. "The most neglected step in the sales process." |
| **Story** | Your brand's origin or methodology story — builds trust, not awareness. |
| **Testimony** | Dedicated step — not an afterthought. Video testimonials > written. CSO structure (Challenge-Solution-Outcome). |
| **Offer** | Clear, specific, risk-reduced. Include bold guarantee if possible. |
| **Response** | Make response frictionless — one click, one action. |

**Content Types:** Case study videos, testimonial compilations, demo walkthroughs.
**Metric That Matters:** Trial signups, consultation bookings, pipeline generation.

### 4.5. Most Aware → Direct Offer (Godfather)

**Sabri Suby's Godfather Offer.** They're ready. Don't educate — close.

| Component | Execution |
|---|---|
| **Offer** | Lead with the offer. Make it too compelling to refuse. |
| **Guarantee** | Bold, specific: *"If [specific result] doesn't happen within [timeframe], [guarantee]."* |
| **Urgency** | Legitimate scarcity or time constraint. Never fabricated. |
| **Social Proof** | Brief — one stat, one testimonial line. They already believe; just reinforce. |

**Content Types:** Direct ads, retargeting, email sequences, landing pages.
**Metric That Matters:** Conversion rate, revenue, CAC.

## 5. Audience Diagnosis Criteria

### 5.1. How to Determine Awareness Level

| Signal | Likely Awareness Level |
|---|---|
| Audience has never heard of the problem or solution category | **Unaware** |
| Audience searches for symptoms, not solutions (*"why is my team slow?"*) | **Problem-Aware** |
| Audience searches for solution categories (*"best project management tools"*) | **Solution-Aware** |
| Audience searches for your brand or compares you to competitors | **Product-Aware** |
| Audience visits pricing page, has used trial, or has been in sales conversation | **Most Aware** |

### 5.2. Funnel Stage Mapping

| Funnel Stage | Awareness Level(s) | Content Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **TOFU** (Top of Funnel) | Unaware, Problem-Aware | Build awareness, create problem recognition |
| **MOFU** (Middle of Funnel) | Solution-Aware, Product-Aware | Differentiate, prove, build trust |
| **BOFU** (Bottom of Funnel) | Product-Aware, Most Aware | Convert, close, retain |

### 5.3. Keyword Signals by Level

| Awareness Level | Search/Behavioral Signals |
|---|---|
| Unaware | Browsing social media, no search intent related to problem |
| Problem-Aware | *"how to fix [symptom]"*, *"why does [pain] happen"*, *"[frustration] solution"* |
| Solution-Aware | *"best [solution category]"*, *"[solution A] vs [solution B]"*, *"how to choose [category]"* |
| Product-Aware | *"[your brand] review"*, *"[your brand] vs [competitor]"*, *"[your brand] pricing"* |
| Most Aware | *"[your brand] discount"*, *"buy [your brand]"*, direct URL visits, cart abandonment |

## 6. Persona × Awareness Cross-Reference

> **Default Awareness Level** = where most individuals in this persona typically sit. **Campaign Target** = the awareness level they're most commonly reached at.

### 6.1. B2B Personas

| Persona | Default Awareness | Most Common Campaign Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| C-Level / Direksi | Product-Aware | Solution-Aware → Product-Aware | Often pre-sold by team; needs strategic framing |
| International Buyer / Investor | Solution-Aware | Solution-Aware → Product-Aware | Evaluate multiple vendors simultaneously |
| Technical Director / Engineer | Solution-Aware | Problem-Aware → Solution-Aware | May enter at problem stage for emerging tech |
| End-User / Operator | Problem-Aware | Problem-Aware → Solution-Aware | Often doesn't know the solution category exists |

### 6.2. EV Personas

| Persona | Default Awareness | Most Common Campaign Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fleet Decision Maker | Product-Aware | Product-Aware | Knows EV fleet is an option; needs TCO proof |
| EV Skeptic | Solution-Aware | Solution-Aware → Product-Aware | Knows EV exists; evaluating readiness |
| Status & Identity Buyer | Problem-Aware | Problem-Aware → Solution-Aware | Feels "sameness" frustration; may not frame EV as the answer |
| Daily Commuter | Problem-Aware | Problem-Aware | Feels fuel pain; may not know EV solves it |
| Corporate ESG Buyer | Product-Aware | Product-Aware | Knows EV transition is needed; evaluating implementation |

## 8. LLM Commandments — Awareness Routing

- **MANDATORY: Determine Awareness Level BEFORE selecting arc, hook, or CTA.** This is Step 0.
- **MANDATORY: Unaware scripts contain ZERO product mentions.** The story IS the product.
- **MANDATORY: Most Aware scripts skip extended storytelling.** Direct offer only.
- **MANDATORY: Match framework to awareness level** per §3 routing table.
- **FORBIDDEN: Using StoryBrand full arc for Unaware audience** — too product-centric.
- **FORBIDDEN: Using soft Transitional CTA for Most Aware audience** — they're ready, close the deal.
- **FORBIDDEN: Serving Problem-Aware audience a comparison/methodology video** — they're not there yet.
