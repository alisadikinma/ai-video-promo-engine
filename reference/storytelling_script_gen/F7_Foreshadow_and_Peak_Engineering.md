# F7_Foreshadow_and_Peak_Engineering.md
## The Two Missing Beats — Open-Loop Retention & Emotional Climax Design
> **Priority:** 🔴 CRITICAL — Without these beats, content captures attention but doesn't retain it (Foreshadow) and doesn't create memory/shareability (Peak).
> **Science:** Zeigarnik Effect (1927) + Kahneman Peak-End Rule (r = 0.581 across 174 studies)

---

## 1. Strategic Context: Why 7 Beats, Not 4

The original StoryBrand-derived arc (Hook → Guide → Plan → CTA) covers 60% of what world-class short-form video requires. Two structural beats are absent:

| Missing Beat | Psychological Mechanism | What It Solves | Drop-Off Data |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Foreshadow** (3–7s) | Zeigarnik Effect — incomplete tasks remembered 2× | Converts *looker* → *watcher*. Answers: "What will I get if I stay?" | 50–60% of all drop-offs occur at 3–7s without this beat |
| **Peak** (60–75%) | Peak-End Rule — memory formed at emotional highpoint | Creates the moment that drives sharing + makes CTA land | Content without a designed peak is 3× less likely to be shared |

**The Revised 7-Beat Arc:**

```
HOOK → FORESHADOW → AGITATE → GUIDE + PLAN → PEAK → CTA → WON DAY
 1         2           3            4            5      6       7
```

## 2. The Foreshadow Beat (3–7 Seconds)

### 2.1. Timing Rules

- **Placement:** Immediately after Hook, always at 3–7 second mark
- **Duration:** 2–5 seconds (shorter in Teasers, longer in Manifestos)
- **Function:** Create an unresolved promise that locks the viewer in
- **Pacing tag:** `[FORESHADOW OPEN]` at start, `[FORESHADOW CLOSE]` at payoff (at or before Peak)

### 2.2. Foreshadow Formula Library (6 Types)

| Code | Formula | Mechanism | Template | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **[A]** | Incomplete Revelation | Reveal part of the insight, withhold the key piece | *"There's one [thing] that [consequence]..."* | *"There's one decision that separates the companies that recovered from the ones that didn't."* |
| **[B]** | Delayed Payoff Preview | Flash the end result, then say "here's how" | *Show result → "Here's how we got there."* | *Visual: Clean dashboard, green metrics. VO: "This is what 60 days of change looks like. Here's what it took."* |
| **[C]** | Story Start Without Finish | Begin a narrative, resolve it at Peak | *"[Timeframe] ago, [person] [situation]..."* | *"Three months ago, a CTO in Jakarta called us with exactly this problem..."* (resolves at Peak) |
| **[D]** | Bold Claim + Delayed Proof | State the result, withhold the method | *"[Stat]. Here's what they changed."* | *"One company cut deployment time from 3 weeks to 2 days. Here's what they changed."* |
| **[E]** | Counter-Intuitive Setup | Say something unexpected, delay the explanation | *"The worst thing you can do is exactly what everyone recommends."* | *"The companies that fixed this didn't spend more. They spent differently."* |
| **[F]** | Before/After Tease | Show transformation briefly, pull back | *Show end-state → "But [timeframe] ago..."* | *"The frustration you feel right now? There's a version of this where it's completely gone."* |

### 2.3. Foreshadow by Video Type

| Video Type | Best Formulas | Timing | Energy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Brand Manifesto** | [A] Incomplete Revelation, [E] Counter-Intuitive | 5–10s mark, 3–5s duration | LOW — philosophical, weighted |
| **Technical Explainer** | [D] Bold Claim, [F] Before/After | 5–10s mark, 3–5s duration | MEDIUM — proof-based |
| **Social Teaser** | [B] Delayed Payoff, [C] Story Start | 3–6s mark, 2–3s duration | HIGH — visual flash-forward |
| **UGC** | [C] Story Start, [A] Incomplete Revelation | 3–7s mark, 2–4s duration | CONVERSATIONAL — incomplete thought |

### 2.4. Foreshadow by Persona

| Persona | Best Formula | Why | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **C-Level** | [D] Bold Claim | They respond to ROI numbers — delayed proof creates executive curiosity | *"One enterprise turned $2.3M in technical debt into their fastest quarter."* |
| **Technical** | [F] Before/After | Engineers want to see the system working — then understand how | *Flash: clean deployment dashboard. "This took 47 days. Here's the 3-step process."* |
| **Operator** | [C] Story Start | Peer narrative is most relatable — unfinished story creates empathy lock | *"A floor supervisor told us last month — her exact words were..."* |
| **Investor** | [A] Incomplete Revelation | Risk they haven't calculated creates anxiety → watch to resolve | *"There's a compliance gap most organizations don't find until the audit."* |
| **Fleet Manager** | [D] Bold Claim | TCO number, delayed proof — board-ready curiosity | *"Fourteen fleet operators ran this audit. Twelve converted within the quarter."* |
| **EV Skeptic** | [E] Counter-Intuitive | Validates their skepticism while opening the loop | *"The smartest move in EV? It was waiting. Until now."* |
| **Status Buyer** | [B] Delayed Payoff | Flash the aspiration — the vehicle in context — withhold access | *Brief shot: vehicle emerging from architectural shadow. No words.* |
| **Daily Commuter** | [F] Before/After | Sensory contrast — morning without vs. morning with | *"Tomorrow morning: full charge, zero stop. Here's what changed."* |
| **ESG Buyer** | [A] Incomplete Revelation | Regulatory gap they haven't measured | *"There's a number missing from 73% of ESG reports this quarter."* |

## 3. The Peak Beat (60–75% of Video Duration)

### 3.1. Timing Rules

- **Placement:** At 60–75% of total video duration, BEFORE CTA
- **Duration:** 5–15 seconds depending on video length
- **Function:** Create the most emotionally intense moment — drives memory, sharing, and CTA momentum
- **Gap Rule:** Maximum **5 seconds** between Peak end and CTA start. No new information in the gap.
- **Pacing tag:** `[PEAK MOMENT]`

### 3.2. Peak Timing Calculator

| Video Duration | Peak Placement (65% default) | Peak Duration | CTA Start |
|---|---|---|---|
| **15 seconds** | 0:09–0:10 | 2s | 0:11 |
| **30 seconds** | 0:18–0:20 | 3s | 0:22 |
| **60 seconds** | 0:38–0:45 | 5–7s | 0:47 |
| **90 seconds** | 0:55–1:05 | 8–10s | 1:08 |
| **180 seconds** | 1:50–2:15 | 12–15s | 2:18 |

### 3.3. Peak Technique Library (5 Types)

| Code | Technique | Execution | Emotional Impact | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **[A]** | Transformation Reveal | The before/after payoff lands — hero SEES the change | Satisfaction + aspiration | Technical Buyer, Operator, Daily Commuter |
| **[B]** | Surprise Data Point | One unexpected stat that reframes everything | Shock + reframe | Investor, ESG Buyer, Fleet Manager, C-Level |
| **[C]** | Music Crescendo + Visual Climax | Sonic + visual sync at emotional apex | Awe + goosebumps | Brand Manifesto, Status Buyer |
| **[D]** | "Aha" Insight | The reframe that changes how hero sees the problem | Intellectual respect + clarity | C-Level, Technical Director |
| **[E]** | Testimonial Climax | Real person's emotional turning point | Trust + identification | All personas — highest proof value |

### 3.4. Peak by Video Type

| Video Type | Primary Technique | Secondary | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Brand Manifesto** | [C] Crescendo + Visual | [D] "Aha" Insight | 55–70% mark, 8–12s |
| **Technical Explainer** | [A] Transformation Reveal | [B] Surprise Data | 60–75% mark, 10–15s |
| **Social Teaser** | [B] Surprise Data | [A] Transformation Reveal | 65–75% mark, 2–4s |
| **UGC** | [E] Testimonial Climax | [A] Transformation Reveal | 60–70% mark, 3–5s |

### 3.5. Peak-to-CTA Bridge Rules

1. **Peak creates momentum. CTA harvests it.** The CTA must ride the emotional wave — not restart the conversation.
2. **Maximum 5 seconds between Peak and CTA.** Longer gap = momentum decay.
3. **No new information between Peak and CTA.** Only the call to act.
4. **The CTA sentence should reference the Peak emotion.** Example: After Transformation Reveal → *"Make this your reality. [CTA action]."*
5. **Won Day image follows CTA.** The final frame shows the hero in their new identity — not the product.

## 4. Foreshadow → Peak Connection (The Macro Loop)

The Foreshadow OPENS a loop. The Peak CLOSES it. This is the single most powerful retention mechanic in short-form video.

| Foreshadow (Opens) | What Viewer Anticipates | Peak (Closes) |
|---|---|---|
| *"One company cut that number by 73%..."* | How? What did they change? | Peak reveals the transformation moment — the exact shift |
| *"A CTO in Jakarta called us with this problem..."* | What happened? Did they fix it? | Peak shows the CTO's system working — clean, fast, trusted |
| *Flash: clean dashboard* | How did they get there? | Peak shows the journey arriving at that dashboard |
| *"There's a number missing from 73% of ESG reports..."* | What number? Am I exposed? | Peak reveals the number — and shows it resolved |

**Quality Gate:** If the Foreshadow opens a loop, the Peak must close it. An unclosed loop = viewer frustration, not satisfaction.

## 5. Micro-Payoff Spacing (Dopamine Architecture)

> **Neuroscience:** Dopamine fires most intensely during **anticipation of reward**, not during the reward itself. This means the Foreshadow (anticipation) is neurologically more engaging than the Peak (payoff).

### 5.1. Spacing Rules

| Zone | Timing | Function | Pacing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hook Zone** | 0–3s | Pattern interrupt + problem stated | Maximum intensity |
| **Foreshadow Zone** | 3–7s | Open loop — anticipation created | Slight deceleration |
| **Body Zone** | 7s – Peak | Micro-payoffs every 3–5 seconds | Steady rhythm |
| **Peak Zone** | 60–75% | Macro-payoff — loop closes | Maximum emotional intensity |
| **CTA Zone** | Post-peak – end | Harvest momentum | Clean, decisive, fast |

### 5.2. Micro-Payoff Types (Deploy Every 3–5 Seconds in Body)

| Type | Execution | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Useful Tip** | One actionable insight the viewer can use immediately | *"The first thing to check is always..."* |
| **Visual Change** | New angle, new graphic, new subject | Cut from screencast to person, or wide to close-up |
| **Humor Beat** | Light moment that breaks analytical mode | Self-deprecating aside, unexpected visual |
| **Small Revelation** | Mini "aha" that closes a mini-loop | *"And that's why the dashboard looks different now."* |
| **Pattern Break** | Unexpected pacing, format, or energy shift | Sudden silence, speed change, text card |

### 5.3. Micro-Payoff Rule

Each micro-payoff **closes a mini-loop** while keeping the **macro Foreshadow loop open**. Think of it as giving the viewer small satisfactions to sustain them until the big payoff at Peak.
