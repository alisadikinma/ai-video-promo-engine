## 1. The 1.7-Second Rule

The average viewer decides in **1.7 seconds** whether to keep watching. This is not a creative guideline — it is a biological constraint. The brain's reticular activating system filters input based on novelty, threat, and relevance. A pattern interrupt bypasses this filter by introducing an unexpected stimulus.

**The Triple-Layer Approach:** Combine one element from each sensory channel within 1.5 seconds for maximum effect:

```
VISUAL (what they see)  +  VERBAL (what they hear/read)  +  AUDIO (what they feel)
         ↓                          ↓                            ↓
   All three fire             within 1.5 seconds              simultaneously
```

## 2. Pattern Interrupt Library — By Sensory Channel

### 2.1. Visual Interrupts

| Technique | Execution | Energy Level | Best For | Platform Fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sudden Zoom** | Cut from WS to ECU in <0.5s, no transition | HIGH | Social Teaser, Reels | TikTok, Reels, Shorts |
| **Mid-Action Start** | Open mid-motion — hand reaching, door opening, screen loading | MEDIUM-HIGH | All types | All platforms |
| **Object Intrusion** | Something enters frame unexpectedly from off-screen | HIGH | Teaser, UGC | TikTok, Reels |
| **Visual Contradiction** | Image doesn't match expected context (luxury item + industrial setting) | MEDIUM | Challenger Hook | All platforms |
| **Bold Text Card** | Large text, high contrast, <4 words, fills screen | HIGH | Sound-off platforms | LinkedIn, Facebook |
| **Extreme Close-Up** | Texture, detail — forces curiosity about context | LOW-MEDIUM | Manifesto, Explainer | YouTube, LinkedIn |
| **Split-Second Flash** | 0.3s flash of Peak/Won Day — too fast to fully process | HIGH | Social Teaser | TikTok, Shorts |
| **Unexpected Scale** | Drone shot where you expected close-up, or vice versa | MEDIUM | Manifesto, Explainer | YouTube, LinkedIn |
| **Motion Against Stillness** | Everything static except one moving element | MEDIUM | Manifesto | LinkedIn, YouTube |
| **Color Pop** | Saturated element in desaturated frame | MEDIUM | Manifesto, Status Buyer | Reels, LinkedIn |

### 2.2. Verbal Interrupts

| Technique | Execution | Energy Level | Best For | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Bold Claim** | Provocative statement, delivered with certainty | HIGH | Data Hook | *"90% of companies get this wrong."* |
| **Direct Address** | Break the fourth wall — speak to viewer personally | MEDIUM-HIGH | UGC, Teaser | *"You — yes, you scrolling past this."* |
| **Mid-Sentence Entry** | Start VO mid-thought, no intro, no setup | HIGH | TikTok, Reels | *"—and that's the part nobody mentions."* |
| **Counter-Intuitive Open** | Say the opposite of what viewer expects | MEDIUM | Challenger Hook | *"Stop trying to innovate."* |
| **Question Start** | Open with a question that demands an answer | MEDIUM | All types | *"What if the reason your AI fails isn't the AI?"* |
| **"Stop" Command** | Literally tell them to stop | HIGH | Social Teaser | *"Stop scrolling. This number is about you."* |
| **Whisper/Conspiratorial** | Lower voice, lean in | LOW-MEDIUM | UGC, Premium | *"Can I tell you something the industry doesn't want you to know?"* |
| **Number First** | Lead with a specific number, no context | HIGH | Data Hook | *"47 times. That's how often you..."* |

### 2.3. Audio Interrupts

| Technique | Execution | Energy Level | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Strategic Silence** | 0.5–1s dead silence before VO begins | LOW-MEDIUM | Manifesto, LinkedIn |
| **Unexpected SFX** | A sound that doesn't belong in context (typewriter in a tech ad) | HIGH | Teaser, Reels |
| **ASMR Element** | Whisper, texture sound, close-mic breathing | LOW | Status Buyer, Premium |
| **Music Genre Shift** | Start with one genre, abruptly switch in first second | HIGH | TikTok, Reels |
| **Single Percussive Hit** | One bass drop or drum hit, then silence | HIGH | All types |
| **Reversed Audio** | 0.5s of reversed sound before forward play | MEDIUM | Social Teaser |
| **Environmental Sound** | Real-world sound that grounds the viewer (traffic, keyboard, factory) | MEDIUM | UGC, Explainer |

## 4. Pattern Interrupt by Persona

| Persona | Recommended Stack | Why | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| **C-Level** | "The Contradiction" or "The Proof" | Intellectual respect; they respond to reframes and data | "The Ambush" — too aggressive for executive attention |
| **Investor** | "The Proof" or "The Freeze" | Risk-sensitive; numbers and authority capture their filter | "The Confession" — too informal for institutional trust |
| **Technical** | "The Shock" (with technical stat) | Engineers respect precision; a specific number stops them | "The Whisper" — too slow for technical curiosity |
| **Operator** | "The Ambush" or "The Confession" | Peer-level energy; they scroll fast, respond to authenticity | "The Freeze" — too philosophical for daily-grind persona |
| **Fleet Manager** | "The Proof" | Cost number on screen = immediate relevance | "The Contradiction" — too abstract for budget-focused mind |
| **EV Skeptic** | "The Freeze" or "The Contradiction" | Validates their careful thinking; intellectual interrupts | "The Ambush" — feels like sales pressure, triggers rejection |
| **Status Buyer** | "The Freeze" or "The Whisper" | Aesthetic, singular; mass-energy repels this persona | "The Shock" — bold text = mass-market signal |
| **Commuter** | "The Ambush" or "The Shock" | They scroll fast; need high-energy to break through | "The Whisper" — too slow for time-poor commuter |
| **ESG Buyer** | "The Proof" | Institutional; numbers and timelines capture attention | "The Confession" — too personal for compliance buyer |

## 5. Dopamine & Micro-Payoff Spacing Architecture

### 5.1. The Neuroscience

Dopamine fires most intensely during **anticipation of reward**, not during the reward itself. This means:

- The **Foreshadow** (anticipation) is neurologically more engaging than the **Peak** (payoff)
- **Micro-payoffs** sustain dopamine between Foreshadow and Peak
- Spacing micro-payoffs every 3–5 seconds maintains the dopamine cycle

### 5.2. Micro-Payoff Types

| Type | What It Is | Example | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Useful Tip** | One actionable insight viewer can use immediately | *"The first thing to check is always the input data."* | 3–5s |
| **Visual Change** | New angle, graphic, subject, or environment | Cut from screencast to person speaking | 0.5–1s transition |
| **Humor Beat** | Light moment that breaks analytical mode | Self-deprecating aside, unexpected visual gag | 2–3s |
| **Small Revelation** | Mini "aha" that closes a mini-loop | *"And that's why the old approach never worked."* | 3–4s |
| **Pattern Break** | Unexpected pacing, format, or energy shift | Sudden silence, speed change, text card | 1–2s |
| **Stat Drop** | Specific number that creates micro-shock | *"73% — that's the gap."* | 2–3s |
| **Proof Flash** | Brief testimonial clip or result screenshot | Customer: *"It took us 2 days instead of 3 weeks."* | 3–5s |

### 5.3. Spacing Rules

| Zone | Timing (in 60s video) | Micro-Payoff Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hook + Foreshadow** | 0–7s | Every 2–3s | Maximum density — establishing hooks |
| **Agitate** | 7–15s | Every 3–4s | Escalation — each payoff deepens the problem |
| **Guide + Plan** | 15–40s | Every 4–5s | Value delivery — each payoff is a learning moment |
| **Pre-Peak Ramp** | 40–45s | Every 2–3s | Acceleration — building toward climax |
| **Peak** | 45–50s | N/A — this IS the macro-payoff | The big moment |
| **CTA + Won Day** | 50–60s | None needed | Harvest momentum — clarity, not stimulation |

### 5.4. The Anti-Pattern: Dead Space

**Dead Space** = any frame/second with no narrative function.

| Dead Space Indicator | Fix |
|---|---|
| Establishing shot >3s without VO or text | Add VO earlier, or compress to <2s |
| B-roll repeating the same idea | Cut to next point |
| VO repeating what text already shows | Remove one or the other |
| Music fill without visual/verbal content | Insert micro-payoff or compress |
| Transition >1s between beats | Tighten or use visual transition from F3 |

**Tolerance:**
- Short-form (≤30s): **Zero** dead space
- Medium-form (30–90s): Maximum **2 seconds** per instance
- Long-form (90s+): Maximum **3 seconds** per instance, maximum **2 instances**
