## 1. Strategic Context: Why EV Requires Its Own Psychological Layer

Standard B2B persona logic targets **organizational** pain. EV promo operates across a more volatile emotional terrain — it simultaneously triggers:

- **Identity anxiety** — *"Am I the kind of person who drives EV?"*
- **Rational skepticism** — *"Is the infrastructure ready? Will I be stranded?"*
- **Moral ambivalence** — *"Do I feel guilty enough to change? Or proud enough to lead?"*

This creates the **EV Dual-Tension Model**: every EV buyer is fighting a war between the *rational objector* (range, charging, price) and the *aspirational self* (status, legacy, planet). The Guide's job is to **collapse that gap** — not with data alone, but with a narrative that makes the aspirational self win.

Additionally, EV marketing must avoid the trap of **Preachy Green-Washing** — moralizing about the environment alienates the rational buyer. Sustainability must be framed as **personal advantage**, not public duty.

## 3. Segment Deep-Dive: The Dual-Tension Narrative Stakes

### 3.1. Fleet Decision Maker — The TCO Dominance Narrative

This buyer presents as rational but decides emotionally: *"Will this make me look competent in the boardroom?"* The external problem (fuel cost) is the symptom. The internal problem is **budget credibility**.

**The Philosophical Injustice:**
> *"A fleet that runs on fuel price volatility is not a fleet — it is a hostage situation. Fleet managers deserve certainty, not a monthly surprise from the pump."*

**Three-Act Narrative Stakes:**

| Act | Beat | Execution |
|---|---|---|
| **I — Hook** | Fuel OPEX as a bleeding wound | Open with a rolling cost counter — every second, the number climbs. No narration. Let the math speak. |
| **II — Guide** | TCO clarity as the antidote | Present the EV TCO model: predictable energy cost + zero unplanned maintenance = budget you can defend |
| **III — Payoff** | Board-room confidence | Close with the Fleet Manager presenting clean Q-over-Q savings — not driving a car, *commanding a strategy*. |

**Identity Transformation:** *"Reactive cost-manager"* → *"Strategic fleet architect."*

### 3.2. EV Skeptic / Rational Objector — The "Proven Right" Narrative

The highest-value and highest-friction persona. They are **not anti-EV** — they are anti-risk. The **worst move** is to argue with their objections. The **correct move** is to make them feel like the smartest person in the room for waiting until now.

**The Philosophical Injustice:**
> *"The market rewarded the hype-buyers first and punished them later. The skeptic who waited — and chose wisely — always wins in the end. That moment is now."*

**Narrative Reframe Table:**

| Their Objection (Shame Layer) | Reframe (Pride Layer) | VO Bridge |
|---|---|---|
| *"What if I run out of charge?"* | *"You planned for this — you chose a vehicle with the route coverage your life actually requires."* | *"Real-world tested on 300+ routes across Southeast Asia."* |
| *"The charging network isn't ready."* | *"You waited until it was. And now it is."* | *"2,400 fast-charge points. Growing by 18 every week."* |
| *"Resale value is uncertain."* | *"First-movers in every technology wave always hold the highest residual value."* | *"Battery warranty. Certified resale programme. Your decision is protected."* |

**Identity Transformation:** *"Cautious observer"* → *"Vindicated early majority."*

### 3.3. Status & Identity Buyer — The Cultural Leadership Narrative

This persona does not buy a vehicle. They **acquire a signal**. The Guide must position the EV not as a trend they are joining, but as a **standard they are setting**.

**The Philosophical Injustice:**
> *"In a world of identical choices, the vehicle you drive is one of the last remaining acts of cultural authorship."*

**Narrative Visual DNA:**

| Scene | Visual Language | Emotional Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Arrival shot | Vehicle emerges from architectural shadow, no crowd | *Singularity* |
| Interior close-up | Hands on steering wheel, city lights reflected | *Control, calm authority* |
| Reaction shot | Others noticing — no dialogue needed | *Recognition without announcement* |
| Final frame | Vehicle alone on an elevated road at dusk | *The road chosen, not inherited* |

**Identity Transformation:** *"Conventional car owner"* → *"Cultural architect of their own era."*

### 3.4. Daily Commuter / Private User — The Invisible Savings Narrative

Time-poor and decision-fatigued. The winning narrative is not about what the EV *has* — it's about what the EV *eliminates*: the gas station ritual, the maintenance anxiety, the monthly fuel bill shock.

**The Philosophical Injustice:**
> *"You work hard enough. Your car should not be another thing that costs you time, money, and energy you don't have."*

**The "Invisible Win" Image Library:**

- **Image of Subtraction:** Phone notification — *"Charging complete."* No station. No queue. No receipt.
- **Image of Flow:** Driving past a gas station — *without stopping*.
- **Image of Discovery:** End-of-month review — savings appeared *without effort*.

**Identity Transformation:** *"Fuel-station dependent commuter"* → *"Person whose car works while they sleep."*

### 3.5. Corporate ESG Buyer — The Audit-Ready Impact Narrative

Under **institutional pressure**, not personal conviction. The EV must function as a **compliance asset**.

**The Philosophical Injustice:**
> *"Organizations that commit to sustainability without the metrics to prove it are not leading — they are exposed."*

| Direct Communication (Internal Approval) | Indirect Communication (Board / Public) |
|---|---|
| Verified Scope 3 reduction per vehicle per year | *"The quarter your fleet went electric is the quarter your ESG report changed permanently."* |
| ISO-aligned emissions baseline documentation | Narrative of a company that moved before the regulation required it |
| Third-party audit compatibility confirmation | Testimonials from peer companies — same industry, same scrutiny |

**Identity Transformation:** *"Compliance-reactive organization"* → *"ESG-proactive market leader."*

## 5. EV-Specific Forbidden Language

| Forbidden Term | Why It Fails | Replace With |
|---|---|---|
| *"eco-friendly"* | Preachy green-washing; repels rational buyers | *"cost-stable," "fuel-independent"* |
| *"the future is electric"* | Cliché — zero narrative transportation | *"the math changed — permanently"* |
| *"zero emissions"* | Abstract; buyer can't feel it | *"Rp X million stays in your account"* |
| *"revolutionary"* | Banned per F2 SOP | *"proven," "verified," "tested"* |
| *"join the movement"* | Collectivist — kills Status Buyer | *"your decision," "your terms"* |
| *"affordable EV"* | Devalues Status Buyer | *"optimized investment," "TCO advantage"* |
| *"green fleet"* | Vague virtue-signal | *"Scope 3-reportable fleet"* |
| Raw kWh / charging minutes | Technical noise for non-engineers | *"charges fully while you sleep"* |

## 6. LLM Prompt Commandments — EV Edition

- **MANDATORY: Deploy the Dual-Tension Arc.** Every script: Shame → Tension Bridge → Pride. No exceptions.
- **MANDATORY: Sustainability = Personal Advantage.** Environmental benefit → time, money, or status saved.
- **MANDATORY: Translate Specs to Feelings.** Range → *"your route, covered."* Battery → *"charges while you sleep."*
- **MANDATORY: Validate the Wait.** For Skeptic personas, reframe hesitation as intelligence.
- **MANDATORY: Check Awareness Level.** Consult §2 matrix. Problem-Aware personas need education before product. Product-Aware need vendor comparison.
- **MANDATORY: Match Proof Type.** Fleet = Outcome. Skeptic = Reliability. Status = Adoption. Commuter = Relevance. ESG = Outcome + Reliability.
- **FORBIDDEN: Preachy Green Narrative.** Environmental duty before personal benefit = rejection.
- **FORBIDDEN: Crowd / Movement Language.** Hero acts alone.
- **FORBIDDEN: Raw Technical Specs Without Translation.**
