## PART A: Tech-to-Value Translation

### A1. The Core Mechanism

Technical buyers don't purchase "speeds and feeds." They invest in resolution of **Internal frustrations** and **Philosophical injustices**. The translation mechanism is a **sense-making bridge**: replace algorithmic inputs with value-outputs that the brain reads as survival-relevant signal — not noise.

**The Architectural Shift to Frame:**

| Traditional AI | Generative AI |
|---|---|
| Classification: *"What is this?"* | Creation: *"What could be?"* |
| Prediction: *"What happens next?"* | Probability: *"What should we build?"* |

Use this contrast to frame the customer's "old world" vs. the "new world" the Guide unlocks.

### A2. Translation Examples (Ready-to-Deploy VO)

| Technical Spec | Internal Problem Solved | Final VO Script |
|---|---|---|
| **Edge computing, 5ms latency** (IoT/Hardware) | Eliminates anxiety of operational lag; removes need for constant manual oversight | *"In a split-second world, you shouldn't have to wait for the cloud to catch up. Our edge technology removes the anxiety of system delays — transforming you from a reactive operator into a strategic leader who commands the floor with total certainty."* |

By grounding our LLM in your proprietary data through RAG, we replace uncertainty with the authority of a data-driven architect. Move from overwhelmed data-seeker to the visionary lead of your company's future."* |

## PART B: The Universal 7-Beat Narrative Arc

> **v4.0 UPGRADE:** Replaces the original 4-beat (Hook → Guide → Plan → CTA) with a 7-beat structure grounded in Zeigarnik Effect, PAS Agitation, and Kahneman's Peak-End Rule.

### B0. The 7-Beat Structure (Master Reference)

```
BEAT 1        BEAT 2          BEAT 3        BEAT 4            BEAT 5      BEAT 6    BEAT 7
HOOK    →   FORESHADOW   →   AGITATE   →   GUIDE + PLAN   →   PEAK    →   CTA   →  WON DAY
(0-3s)       (3-7s)          (7-12s)       (12-25s)         (60-75%)    (last 5s)  (final frame)

Pattern      Open Loop        Escalate      Empathy +         Emotional   Action    Identity
Interrupt    (Zeigarnik)      Pain (PAS)    Authority +       Climax      Harvest   Arrival
                                            3-Step Plan       (Peak-End)
```

#### 6-Stage User Framework Alias (v2.2.0+)

User-facing brainstorm + brief uses 6-stage terminology that aliases to the 7-beat internal IDs:

```
HOOK → Foreshadow → BODY 1 (Problems) → BODY 2 (Solutions) → Peak → Ending + CTA
```

| User-Facing Beat | Internal 7-Beat ID |
|---|---|
| HOOK | Pattern Interrupt + HOOK |
| Foreshadow | FORESHADOW |
| **BODY 1 (Problems)** | **AGITATE** |
| **BODY 2 (Solutions)** | **GUIDE + PLAN** |
| Peak | PEAK |
| Ending + CTA | CTA + WON DAY |

**BODY 1 Completeness Hard Rule:** count(pains dramatized in BODY 1 scenes) ≥ count(pains identified in Phase 1 brainstorm). Pairing allowed for pains with shared root cause. See `global-promo-config.md` §25 for full rule + auto-fail triggers + validator C1.

| Beat | Psychological Mechanism | What It Does | If Missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Hook** | Pattern Interrupt — breaks autopilot | Stops the scroll, creates "why should I look?" | No attention captured |
| **2. Foreshadow** | Zeigarnik Effect — unfinished tasks remembered 2× | Creates "what will I get if I stay?" — converts looker → watcher | 50-60% drop-off at 3-7s mark |
| **3. Agitate** | PAS Escalation — amplifies emotional discomfort | Makes the problem FEEL unbearable before solution appears | Solution feels optional, not urgent |
| **4. Guide + Plan** | StoryBrand — empathy + authority + 3-step path | Provides hope, reduces perceived risk, shows the way forward | Hero sees no path, stays paralyzed |
| **5. Peak** | Peak-End Rule (r=0.581) — memory formed at emotional highpoint | Creates the moment that makes content memorable and shareable | Forgettable content, weak CTA momentum |
| **6. CTA** | Inciting Incident — prevents hero from remaining in stasis | Harvests the emotional momentum from Peak into action | No conversion |
| **7. Won Day** | Identity Visualization — hero sees their future self | Final image of who hero becomes after saying yes | No emotional closure |

### B1. Macro-to-Micro Arc — C-Level & Investors

Start with **Philosophical Stakes** → resolve to **Visionary Payoff**.

| Act | Beat | Execution |
|---|---|---|
| **I — Hook** | External + Philosophical problem | Frame stagnant growth as a moral injustice: *"Enterprises deserve to scale without being throttled by legacy technical debt."* |
| **I-b — Foreshadow** | Open loop / bold claim | Flash the transformation: *"One company turned $2.3M in technical debt into their fastest quarter."* Withhold the how. |
| **I-c — Agitate** | Escalate the philosophical stakes | Future-cast consequences: *"Every quarter you delay, the gap doesn't shrink — it compounds. Your competitors aren't waiting."* |
| **II — Guide + Plan** | Systemic solution roadmap | Brand enters with Empathy + Authority. Present high-level strategic roadmap — no feature lists. |
| **II-b — Desire** | Aspirational visualization | Paint the visceral want: *"Imagine presenting Q4 results where every dollar of tech investment maps to revenue growth."* |
| **III — Peak** | Transformation reveal / data climax | The "aha" moment — the single insight or proof point that makes the case undeniable. |
| **III-b — CTA + Won Day** | Visionary payoff | CTA harvests Peak momentum. Close on Won Day: Market Dominance → Operational Cohesion → Investor ROI trajectory. |

**Identity Transformation Goal:** Organization moves from *"confused observer"* → *"market architect."*

### B2. Micro-to-Macro Arc — Technical Directors & Operators

Start with **Internal daily friction** → resolve to **Strategic identity upgrade**.

| Act | Beat | Execution |
|---|---|---|
| **I — Hook** | Internal struggle | Validate frustration: tokens they can't control, weights they can't adjust, outputs they can't trust. |
| **I-b — Foreshadow** | Before/after tease | Show the end state briefly — a clean, working system — then pull back: *"Here's how we got there."* |
| **I-c — Agitate** | Ripple-effect mapping | Show how the daily friction cascades: one failed deployment → missed sprint → eroded team trust → leadership doubt. |
| **II — 3-Step Plan** | Path of Hope | **Ingest** proprietary data → **Refine** via RLHF → **Generate** grounded outputs. |
| **II-b — Desire** | "Imagine this working" moment | *"Imagine running inference on your own data and trusting the output — the first time, every time."* |
| **III — Peak** | Transformation reveal | The system working — clean, fast, trusted. The operator's face shifts from doubt to command. |
| **III-b — CTA + Won Day** | Long-term scalability | Operator transforms from *"Passive Troubleshooter"* → *"Strategic Architect"* of the AI future. |

**Alignment Gap Rule:** Deploy both arcs across a video library. C-level ROI vision and operator efficiency must be **two sides of the same success story** — never siloed.

## PART C: Video Typology & Pacing Parameters

### C1. Type I — Brand Manifesto (The Macro-Arc)

**For:** C-Level Executives & Investors
**Duration:** 60–90 seconds
**Pacing:** SLOW — deploy `[PAUSE]` tags after every Philosophical One-Liner. Silence = Authority.
**Structural Focus:** Philosophical Problem → Foreshadow → Agitate → Aspirational Identity → Peak → CTA

**7-Beat Timing (90s):**

| Beat | Time | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | 0:00–0:05 | 5s |
| Foreshadow | 0:05–0:10 | 5s |
| Agitate | 0:10–0:20 | 10s |
| Guide + Plan | 0:20–0:50 | 30s |
| Peak | 0:55–1:10 | 15s |
| CTA | 1:10–1:20 | 10s |
| Won Day | 1:20–1:30 | 10s |

**Production Ratio:**

| Component | Weight | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| VISUAL | 60% | Cinematic, atmospheric; visual proof of Aspirational Identity |
| AUDIO | 40% | Sparse, impactful narration; Empathy + Authority; heavy `[PAUSE]` usage |

### C2. Type II — Technical Explainer (Tech-to-Value Translation)

**For:** Technical Directors & Professional End-Users
**Duration:** 2–3 minutes
**Pacing:** MEDIUM — clear visual transitions between feature sets to prevent cognitive overload.
**Structural Focus:** Micro (3-Step Process Plan) → Desire → Peak (Success vs. Failure stakes)
**CTA Type:** Transitional ("Let's Date") — whitepaper, sandbox, or PoC demo.

**7-Beat Timing (150s):**

| Beat | Time | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | 0:00–0:05 | 5s |
| Foreshadow | 0:05–0:10 | 5s |
| Agitate | 0:10–0:25 | 15s |
| Guide + Plan | 0:25–1:20 | 55s |
| Desire | 1:20–1:35 | 15s |
| Peak | 1:35–1:55 | 20s |
| CTA + Won Day | 1:55–2:30 | 35s |

**Production Ratio:**

| Component | Weight | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| VISUAL | 50% | High-density info; screencasts + diagrams illustrating Process Plan |
| AUDIO | 50% | Detailed narration; solving Internal Problem (frustration/overwhelm) |

### C3. Type III — Social Teaser / Ad (The Aggressive Hook)

**For:** Universal / Top-of-Funnel Prospects
**Duration:** 15–30 seconds
**Pacing:** FAST/AGGRESSIVE — use `[QUICK CUT]` tags; high-energy audio throughout.
**Structural Focus:** Hook → Foreshadow → compressed body → Peak → Direct CTA.

**7-Beat Timing (30s):**

| Beat | Time | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Pattern Interrupt + Hook | 0:00–0:03 | 3s |
| Foreshadow | 0:03–0:06 | 3s |
| Agitate (compressed) | 0:06–0:10 | 4s |
| Guide + Plan (compressed) | 0:10–0:18 | 8s |
| Peak | 0:18–0:22 | 4s |
| CTA + Won Day | 0:22–0:30 | 8s |

**Loopable Variant (YouTube Shorts optimization):**
End flows seamlessly back into beginning — final frame's visual/audio connects to first frame. Pushes average view duration above 100%.

**Production Ratio:**

| Component | Weight | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| VISUAL | 70% | High-velocity, scroll-stopping visuals for silent environments |
| AUDIO | 30% | Punchy, rhythmic copy; External Problem + Direct CTA only |

### C4. Type IV — UGC-Style Direct-to-Camera (NEW)

> **v4.0 ADDITION.** UGC-style content is 22% more effective than brand-created video on TikTok and delivers 8× better performance when shared by actual customers.

**For:** Problem-Aware & Solution-Aware audiences / All personas at TOFU-MOFU
**Duration:** 15–60 seconds
**Pacing:** CONVERSATIONAL — natural speech rhythm, minimal cuts, authentic feel.
**Structural Focus:** All 7 beats embedded in casual direct-to-camera format.

**Production Rules — "Imperfection-as-Trust" Aesthetic:**

| Parameter | UGC Rule | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Camera | Smartphone, handheld, slight movement OK | Polished = ad = skip |
| Lighting | Natural / ring light. No cinematic setup. | Authenticity signal |
| Script Format | Bullet-point outline, NOT word-for-word | Natural delivery = trust |
| Location | Office, home, warehouse floor — real environments | Context = credibility |
| Graphics | Minimal — text overlay only for key stats | Over-production kills UGC trust |
| VO Tone | Peer-to-peer, conversational, first-person | "Someone like me" effect |
| Duration | 15–45s optimal | Longer UGC loses authenticity |

**7-Beat Adaptation for UGC:**

| Beat | UGC Execution |
|---|---|
| Hook | Mid-sentence start, direct address: *"OK so here's the thing nobody tells you about..."* |
| Foreshadow | Incomplete revelation: *"And the part that shocked me..."* (continues later) |
| Agitate | Personal story: *"I spent three months trying to fix this manually..."* |
| Guide + Plan | *"Then I found this approach — three steps..."* |
| Peak | Personal transformation moment: *"And the first time it worked, I literally..."* |
| CTA | *"Link in bio" / "Drop a comment if you want the template"* |
| Won Day | *"Now I don't even think about it anymore."* |

**Forbidden in UGC:**
- Scripted corporate language
- Professional lighting/camera rigs
- Logo watermarks in first 5s
- Multiple camera angles (feels produced)
- Teleprompter delivery (eyes give it away)

## PART E: Unified SOP Guardrails

### E1. Non-Negotiable Content Rules

1. **Hero/Guide Axiom** — Customer = Hero. Brand = Guide. Brand never competes for the spotlight. *(Canonical: F1)*
2. **Triple-Threat Requirement** — Every script resolves **External** (tangible) + **Internal** (emotional) + **Philosophical** (moral) problems.
3. **Empowerment Only** — Forbid "Inadequacy Marketing." Call the audience to a higher identity; never shame them into action.
4. **3-Step Plan Maximum** — More than 3 steps = the brain perceives a new problem, not a solution.
5. **"We/Us/Our" Blackout** — Strictly forbidden in the first 10 seconds. Customer must be the grammatical subject.
6. **Sell to Internal Problems** — Don't fix the leaky pipe (External); resolve the Hero's anxiety (Internal). People buy to change how they feel.
7. **7-Beat Compliance** — Every script must contain all 7 beats: Hook, Foreshadow, Agitate, Guide+Plan, Peak, CTA, Won Day. *(v4.0)*
8. **Awareness-First Routing** — Consult F8 before selecting arc or video type. *(v4.0)*
9. **False Belief Breaking** — When deploying Challenger Hook, explicitly identify and refute the hero's limiting belief (Brunson HSO). *(v4.0)*

### E2. Pacing Metadata Tag Reference

| Tag | When to Use |
|---|---|
| `[PAUSE – 3s]` | After Philosophical Problem or high-stakes One-Liner (Manifesto only) |
| `[QUICK CUT]` | Between every beat in Social Teaser; drives momentum |
| `[VISUAL TRANSITION]` | Between feature sets in Technical Explainer; prevents overload |
| `[30Hz SUB-BASS DROP]` | Marks Problem-to-Plan transition; provides physical authority cue |
| `[FORESHADOW OPEN]` | Marks the Foreshadow beat — open loop begins *(v4.0)* |
| `[FORESHADOW CLOSE]` | Marks where the open loop pays off (at or before Peak) *(v4.0)* |
| `[PEAK MOMENT]` | Marks the designed emotional climax — 60-75% of duration *(v4.0)* |
| `[AGITATE]` | Marks PAS escalation beat — emotional discomfort amplified *(v4.0)* |

### E3. Prohibited Output

The AI is **forbidden** from generating the following — treat as automatic rejection signals:

- Words: *synergy, leverage, robust, revolutionary, cutting-edge, seamlessly*
- Structure: Feature list without a mapped Internal Problem
- Opening: Brand as subject in first 10 seconds
- Claims: Success assertions not grounded in Profitability, Team Confidence, or Time/Money Savings
- Arc: Script with no Foreshadow beat (viewer drops at 3-7s) *(v4.0)*
- Arc: Script with no Peak beat (content not memorable, weak CTA momentum) *(v4.0)*
- Arc: CTA without preceding Peak (no emotional momentum to harvest) *(v4.0)*
- Pacing: Dead space > 2 seconds in short-form content *(v4.0)*
